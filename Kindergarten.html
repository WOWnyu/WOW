Deserts

• Deserts provide a habitat for a variety of living things.
• Deserts are important for the life cycle of various plants/animals.
• Deserts help some animals get their necessary food, water, and protection.

Space

• Observable objects in space (sun, moon, etc.) affect plant & animal life on Earth.
• Planets in space orbit around the sun impacting weather & climate.
• Predictable patterns in space can be observes and used to benefit humans.

Next Generation Science Standards

Wild Weather

• Sun’s warming relates to weather, including wild weather.
• Fronts occurring when air masses meet can create wild weather.
• Wild weather impacts society and ecosystems.

Motion

• The world is full of motion.
• Motion impacts every aspect of life on Earth and in outer space.

Seasons

• Seasons impact the behavior of living things around the world.
• Humans prepare for the seasons in different ways, some of those ways are based on tradition and culture.

Patterns

• The world is full of patterns.
• Patterns can help us to make predictions.
• Many patterns have a purpose.
• Patterns can be observed in nature.
• Some patterns show growth or age.
• Patterns can be used by some animals for protection (camouflage).


Oceans

• Oceans provide a habitat for a variety of living things.
• Oceans are important for the life cycles of various plants/animals.
• Oceans are used by living things for food, minerals, water, and protection.
• Oceans impact out atmosphere and provide oxygen.
• There are 5 Oceans on the Earth, which are all joined together.

Next Generation Science Standards

K-ESS2-2 K-ESS3-1 K-ESS3-3




Shadows and Light

• The sun affects what we experience on Earth, such as creating shadows and light.
• Heavenly bodies, as they move in space, change the positioning of shadows and light hitting the Earth.
• Light interacts differently with objects depending on distance, angles or the nature of the object, affecting shadows.
• Understanding shadows and light helps humans in many areas of life (astronomy, gardening, solar energy, etc.).

Next Generation Science Standards

K-PS3-1



Rainforests

• Rainforests provide a habitat for a variety of living things.
• Rainforests are important for the life cycles of various plants/animals.
• Rainforests help some animals get their necessary food, water, and protection.
• Rainforests are used by living things for food, medicine, and protections.
• Rainforest are an important source for providing basic needs for humans, including food, medicine, and other resources.
• Rainforests impact our atmosphere and provide oxygen.

Next Generation Science Standards

Shapes

• The world is full of shapes.
• Shapes can be observed in various aspects of life on Earth and in outer space, naturally and manmade.
• Shapes can be beneficial for human use.
